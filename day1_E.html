Ramp Safety Training – Day 1

Welcome to your first day of Ramp Safety training. Please read the material carefully.

What is Ramp Safety?

Ramp safety involves all practices and procedures necessary to maintain a safe environment on the airport ramp where aircraft are parked, loaded, unloaded, refueled, or maintained. Safety awareness on the ramp helps prevent accidents, injuries, and operational delays.

Common Hazards on the Ramp

  • Jet blast and propeller wash
  • Moving vehicles and ground equipment
  • Fuel spills and chemical exposure
  • Slip, trip, and fall risks

Personal Protective Equipment (PPE)

Always wear the required PPE while on the ramp. This typically includes:

  • High-visibility vest
  • Hearing protection
  • Steel-toe boots
  • Safety goggles (as needed)

Ramp Safety Do's and Don'ts

Do:

  • Be aware of your surroundings at all times.
  • Follow marked pathways and designated routes.
  • Report unsafe conditions immediately.

Don't:

  • Use mobile phones while walking on the ramp.
  • Ignore warning signs or restricted areas.
  • Approach aircraft without clearance.
